CRITERIA (verbatim) "Sepsis was identified by the ICD-9-CM and ICD-10-CM codes proposed by Angus et al., in which a diagnosis of bacterial or fungal infection and at least one acute organ dysfunction are needed to define a septic episode. The definition of acute organ dysfunction comprised: dysfunction of the respiratory, cardiovascular, hepatic, hematologic, renal, and/or central nervous system; metabolic dysfunction; and cardiovascular shock."
CASE / CONTROL Cohort study (single exposed cohort of all TNFi initiators). No matched case-control design. Risk factor analysis performed within the cohort using Cox regression.
OUTCOME First diagnosis of sepsis after TNFi initiation (index date = first ED or hospital visit for sepsis). Censored at 31 Dec 2017, death, or NHI withdrawal.
INTERVENTION TNFi therapy: etanercept, adalimumab, or golimumab. (Infliximab & certolizumab excluded — approved in Taiwan 2017.)
VARIABLES (key) Age, sex, CCI, recent sepsis (3 mo pre-TNFi), IMID type (RA, AS, PsO, PsA, CD, UC), TNFi type, concomitant DMARDs (MTX, SSZ, HCQ, LEF, immunosuppressants), glucocorticoid dose (prednisolone-equivalent mg/day), urbanization level (I–IV), insured amount (payroll quartiles Q1–Q4).
STATISTICS Descriptive (mean±SD, %). Incidence rate per 100,000 person-years. Cox proportional hazards regression (univariable + multivariable). HR + 95% CI. Adjusted for age, sex, CCI and other confounders. SAS 9.3. p < 0.05 significant.
PURPOSES "To investigate the incidence of sepsis and to identify risk factors for sepsis in patients receiving TNFi for IMIDs" using Sepsis-3 definition in the Taiwanese NHIRD.
RESULTS (ALL KEY FINDINGS) Overall IR = 1,088 / 100,000 py. Highest in CD (2,509), UC (1,520), RA (1,262). Golimumab lower IR vs etanercept (IRR 0.6 in RA). Multivariable HRs: recent sepsis 2.35 (1.73–3.20), CD 3.36 (2.11–5.34 vs AS), glucocorticoid dose 1.05/mg/day (1.05–1.06), golimumab 0.56 (0.38–0.83 vs etanercept), urbanization level IV 1.69 (1.35–2.12), insured Q4 0.32 (0.19–0.52). Sulfasalazine protective (HR 0.81). Dose-response SES gradient observed.
CONCLUSION "Recent sepsis, CD, concomitant glucocorticoid use, and low socioeconomic status, which were associated with an increased risk of sepsis, are crucial for individualized risk management plans."
INNOVATION First nationwide study applying Sepsis-3 definition to TNFi-treated IMIDs. Identified golimumab signal, strong CD risk, and socioeconomic gradient in universal-coverage setting. Real-world evidence for precision risk stratification.
LIMITATION "First, ... data on drug compliance could not be assessed... laboratory data, including disease activity and microbiological findings, could not be assessed... The lack of data regarding disease activity in claim data is also a limitation... the risk for viral infections as well as mycobacterial infections... were not assessed."

TASK 2: Critical Appraisal & Analysis (with verbatim excerpts)
1 Value (val) — Why this study was publishable in this journal
This study was highly publishable because it filled a critical evidence gap using a rigorous, modern definition (Sepsis-3) in a large, unselected nationwide cohort of TNFi-treated patients across multiple IMIDs. The journal (Therapeutic Advances in Musculoskeletal Disease) focuses on practical, real-world advances in rheumatology; this paper directly informs individualized risk management — a clinically urgent topic given the well-known infection risk of biologics. The identification of golimumab as potentially protective, the striking CD signal, and the socioeconomic gradient (even within universal healthcare) provided novel, actionable insights beyond existing meta-analyses of "serious infections."
Verbatim excerpt (Abstract):
"Risk factors for sepsis have not been assessed in patients receiving tumor necrosis factor-alpha inhibitors (TNFi) for immune-mediated inflammatory diseases (IMIDs) who are vulnerable to serious/hospitalized infections... These data provide real-world evidence of sepsis risk in patients treated with distinct TNFi for IMIDs and highlight the need for individualized risk management strategies."
2 What the study aimed to do (wtd)
The overarching objective was to quantify the incidence of sepsis (using the 2016 Sepsis-3 definition) and identify independent risk factors among patients initiating TNFi for any of six IMIDs in a real-world Asian population. The authors explicitly moved beyond vague "serious infection" endpoints used in prior trials and meta-analyses.
Verbatim excerpt (Introduction):
"Therefore, we aimed to assess the sepsis risk in patients receiving TNFi for IMIDs using the Sepsis-3 definition. In the present study, we used the Taiwanese Nationwide Health Insurance (NHI) Research Database (NHIRD) to investigate the incidence of sepsis and to identify risk factors for sepsis in patients receiving TNFi for IMIDs."
3 Summary of previous literature (spl)
Prior literature established that TNFi increase serious infection risk (Singh Bayesian NMA, OR 1.31–1.90). However, definitions were heterogeneous (hospitalization, IV antibiotics, death). Data were strongest for RA; relatively sparse for AS, PsO, PsA and especially IBD. Socioeconomic determinants and history of recent sepsis were rarely examined in biologic users.
Verbatim excerpt (Discussion):
"TNFi is associated with an increased risk of infection with varied definitions. However, the definite sepsis risk in patients receiving TNFi for IMIDs remains unclear. Singh et al. conducted a Bayesian network meta-analysis... standard-dose biological drugs (odds ratio, 1.31; 95% CI 1.09–1.58)..."
4 Critique of previous literature (cpl)
Previous studies suffered from inconsistent infection definitions, limited generalizability beyond RA, and failure to apply organ-dysfunction-based criteria (Sepsis-3). Many relied on trial populations with strict inclusion criteria, missing real-world channelling bias and socioeconomic gradients. The authors correctly note that Sepsis-2 criteria are unreliable.
Verbatim excerpt (Discussion):
"The previous sepsis definition, Sepsis-2... has been found to be somewhat unreliable. One in eight patients admitted to an intensive care unit with infection and new organ failure are estimated to not fulfil the sepsis definition according to Sepsis-2..."
5 Gap between the known and unknown (gap)
No prior study had specifically quantified sepsis incidence (Sepsis-3) or comprehensively examined risk factors (including recent sepsis history, IMID subtype differences, TNFi molecule differences, and socioeconomic status) in a nationwide cohort of TNFi-treated patients spanning RA, SpA and IBD.
Verbatim excerpt (Introduction):
"Risk factors for sepsis have not been assessed in patients receiving tumor necrosis factor-alpha inhibitors (TNFi) for immune-mediated inflammatory diseases (IMIDs) who are vulnerable to serious/hospitalized infections."
6 Rationale for filling the gap (rat)
A large claims database with universal coverage allowed unbiased estimation of incidence and risk factors. Sepsis-3 provides a clinically meaningful, reproducible endpoint. Identifying modifiable (glucocorticoid dose, recent sepsis) and non-modifiable (CD, low SES) risk factors enables targeted monitoring and shared decision-making.
Verbatim excerpt (Conclusion):
"These findings provide real-world evidence of specific risk factors of sepsis in TNFi-treated patients with IMIDs, which warrant further studies to establish individualized risk management strategies in patients receiving TNFi for IMIDs."
7 What they did in this study (wtdd) — Methodology
Retrospective cohort of all patients starting etanercept, adalimumab or golimumab for RA/AS/PsO/PsA/CD/UC in NHIRD 2003–2017 (n=17,764). Sepsis defined by Angus ICD codes + organ dysfunction. Person-time incidence calculated. Multivariable Cox models with extensive adjustment. Sensitivity analyses implicit via stratification by IMID and TNFi.
Verbatim excerpt (Methods — Outcome):
"The study outcome was the first diagnosis with sepsis after the initiation of TNFi. In the present study, we employed the Sepsis-3 definition to identify patients with sepsis... The index date of sepsis was defined as the first day of an emergency department or hospital visit for sepsis."
8 Results consistent with previous literature (rcl)
Findings align with known higher infection risk in RA and IBD on TNFi, dose-dependent glucocorticoid risk, older age, higher CCI, and increased recurrent sepsis risk after recent sepsis (consistent with Prescott Medicare and Shen NHIRD studies). Incidence in CD (2,509/100k) closely mirrored French IBD data (1,890/100k serious infections).
Verbatim excerpt (Discussion):
"Using the Medicare claim database... Prescott et al. found that patients were frequently re-hospitalized within the first 90 days after severe sepsis... Similarly, in a study of the Taiwanese NHIRD, Shen et al. reported that patients who survived hospitalization for sepsis had a higher risk of subsequent sepsis..."
9 Results contrary to previous literature (rtc)
Novel signals include: (1) golimumab associated with ~44% lower sepsis risk vs etanercept (not previously shown in head-to-head); (2) CD carried markedly higher risk than RA (HR 3.36); (3) strong, graded socioeconomic effect on sepsis despite universal NHI coverage — a finding under-appreciated in prior biologic safety literature.
Verbatim excerpt (Results & Discussion):
"Intriguingly, golimumab users appeared to have a lower risk of sepsis compared with etanercept users (HR, 0.56; 95% CI, 0.38–0.83)... The sepsis risk was greatest in patients with the lowest level (IV) of urbanization (HR, 1.69; 95% CI, 1.35–2.12)..."
10 Recommendations for future research (rfw)
The authors recommend vigilance for recurrent sepsis in recent sepsis survivors, consideration of urban-rural and income disparities when initiating TNFi, and further validation of the golimumab signal. They implicitly support steroid-sparing strategies and individualized monitoring plans.
Verbatim excerpt (Discussion):
"These lines of evidence indicate the importance of vigilance for recurrent sepsis in patients treated with TNFi for IMIDs who have recently recovered from sepsis... the association between living in less urbanized areas and the increased sepsis risk... suggests that urban-rural disparities across Taiwan should be considered while administering TNFi..."
11 Points of critique (flaws & limitations) (poc)
Major limitations include reliance on claims data (no disease activity scores, no microbiologic confirmation, no drug level or compliance data), exclusion of infliximab/certolizumab, potential channelling bias (etanercept users had higher comorbidity burden), and inability to assess viral or mycobacterial infections. The short look-back for "recent sepsis" (3 months) may underestimate longer-term vulnerability.
Verbatim excerpt (Discussion — Limitations):
"The present study has several limitations. First, given that the study focused on the sepsis risk among patients receiving TNFi, the risk for viral infections as well as mycobacterial infections and the role of biologics other than TNFi were not assessed. Second, data on drug compliance could not be assessed in the NHIRD..."
12 Missed obvious points (mop)
The authors did not compare sepsis rates to the general Taiwanese population or to IMID patients not receiving TNFi. They also did not explore whether the protective golimumab signal persists after adjustment for channeling (etanercept users were sicker). No formal interaction testing between TNFi type and IMID subtype was presented. Disease duration was shorter in etanercept users — a potential unmeasured confounder.
13 Relevant points worth pursuing (rpp)
Prospective validation of golimumab vs other TNFi regarding sepsis; mechanistic studies of why CD patients have disproportionately high risk (intra-abdominal source?); targeted interventions (closer monitoring, vaccination, steroid minimization) for recent-sepsis and low-SES subgroups; integration with tapering strategies (see SORAIRO trial data) to balance infection risk vs disease control; cost-effectiveness of risk-stratified monitoring.
14 Order of reasoning in the discussion (oor)
The discussion follows a logical clinical narrative: (1) summary of main findings, (2) detailed discussion of the strongest predictor (recent sepsis) with supporting external literature, (3) justification for Sepsis-3 over prior definitions, (4) comparison of incidence rates to existing serious-infection literature (Singh, Quartuccio, Kirchgessner French IBD), (5) explanation of high CD risk (intra-abdominal infections), (6) intriguing golimumab signal and call for validation, (7) socioeconomic findings with external corroboration, (8) transparent limitations, and (9) concise clinical implications.
