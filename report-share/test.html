Background & Rationale

Idiopathic pulmonary arterial hypertension (IPAH) is a clinical syndrome diagnosed by exclusion. Significant heterogeneity exists within the diagnosis.

  • Patients with identical RV afterload may or may not develop RV failure
  • Current risk tools (Euro-score, French, REVEAL) incompletely capture phenotype diversity
  • Treatment response remains highly variable despite modern therapies
  • Cluster analysis of clinical variables can reveal clinically meaningful subtypes

Aim: Apply hierarchical cluster analysis to improve phenotyping and relate clusters to long-term clinical worsening.

Traditional Risk Scores vs Clusters

Current tools (European average, French low-risk count, REVEAL) show substantial overlap across clusters and fail to mirror the large outcome differences.

  • Most patients in every cluster fall into the intermediate-risk category
  • Low-risk patients are still present in Cluster 3 (worst outcome)
  • c-statistic of clustering model ≈ 0.61 – similar to risk scores, yet provides orthogonal phenotypic information
  • Highlight: need to incorporate RV imaging + CPET parameters into risk assessment

Key Findings

  • Four reproducible clinical phenotypes exist inside the single diagnostic label “IPAH”
  • Combination of mPAP + RVEDA + O₂ pulse is sufficient to stratify patients
  • Cluster 3 (severe PH + maladaptive RV) carries a 13.8-fold higher risk of clinical worsening versus Cluster 1
  • Traditional risk scores do not adequately separate these outcome trajectories
  • Multidimensional phenotyping (echo + CPET) is required for precision treatment decisions

Clinical & Research Implications

  • Treatment intensity should be phenotype-guided rather than solely risk-score-guided
  • Cluster 1: possible monotherapy or treatment de-escalation
  • Cluster 2: dual oral combination (AMBITION-style)
  • Cluster 3: early consideration of parenteral prostanoids
  • Cluster 4: structured exercise rehabilitation programs
  • Future trials should enrich or stratify by these phenotypes to reduce heterogeneity of treatment effect

Conclusions

Cluster analysis of routinely available clinical, hemodynamic, echocardiographic and CPET variables identifies four distinct, prognostically relevant IPAH phenotypes.

These findings underscore the high degree of disease heterogeneity within IPAH and the necessity of advanced phenotyping to refine risk assessment and personalize therapy.
